# Marketing Notes

Working positioning, copy bank, and channels. Living document.

## Positioning

> **An OS for the people who'd rather own a company than work at one.**

One-line variants for different audiences:

- For founders: *"Run a 10-person company with one person."*
- For creators: *"Get your weekends back without losing your audience."*
- For families: *"Take Fridays off without dropping clients."*
- For builders: *"The substrate for the agentic businesses of the next decade."*

## Anti-positioning

These are the things we explicitly **don't** want to be:

- Not "the agent framework with the prettiest API."
- Not "another no-code workflow builder with a chatbot bolted on."
- Not "a productivity app that replaces your Notion."
- Not "an AI assistant that does what ChatGPT does, but in a window."

## The hero promise

Three lines, in order:

1. **Tell us what you want.** ("Reach 1,000 newsletter subscribers by July.")
2. **We hire the agents.** Paperclip plans, Hermes writes, OpenClaw clicks.
3. **Wake up to a status update.** Approve, redirect, or do nothing.

## Copy bank — tag-line shape

Use these exact rhythms for headlines, hero sections, ads:

- "Work is for bots. **Life is for humans.**"
- "Hire your first agent today. **Sleep better tonight.**"
- "Set a goal. **Go to dinner.**"
- "A company runs itself. **You run your life.**"
- "Your second hire. **Without a second salary.**"

## What goes on www.blankcollar.ai (Phase 8 plan)

Above the fold:
- Tag-line ("Work is for bots. Life is for humans.")
- One sentence sub-line.
- Single CTA: *"Start your agent company."*
- A 20-second loop showing: a goal typed → a plan generated → a status email arriving Friday.

Below the fold:
- The three-step promise.
- Three named templates ("Solo Creator", "Two-Person SaaS", "Family Agency") — clickable.
- A "What you keep doing yourself" section (calming, not scary).
- Pricing — flat, no enterprise tier, free self-host always.
- Footer with manifesto link.

## What stays out

- Stock photos of people pointing at laptops.
- "Powered by AI" badges.
- Testimonials we don't have yet.
- Vague stats ("10x faster!").
- Anything that mentions disruption.

## Channels we'll bias toward

- Long-form essays from Kristian on www.theblankcollar.com — slow, opinionated, narrative.
- Twitter/X threads tied to those essays.
- A weekly "Agent Diary" newsletter — what one of *our* agents actually did this week.
- Selective podcast appearances when the product is real.

## Channels we'll skip

- Programmatic ads.
- Cold outbound.
- Paid sponsorships before the product can carry conversation.

## Launch sequence (placeholder, Phase 8)

1. Open-source repo public.
2. Manifesto post on theblankcollar.com.
3. www.blankcollar.ai with a waitlist.
4. Three template invites to private beta (Solo Creator, SaaS, Agency).
5. Public open with hosted tier + free self-host.
