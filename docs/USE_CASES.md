# Use Cases

Concrete pictures of who runs Blank Collar and what they have it do. Useful as design fuel, marketing fuel, and a reality check on the roadmap.

Each scenario is told as: **Who → Goal → Departments → What the OS does → What stays human.**

---

## 1. The Solo Newsletter Operator

**Who:** A writer with a 4,000-subscriber newsletter and a day job they want to leave.

**Top-level goal:** *"Reach 25,000 subscribers and $5k MRR by year-end."*

**Departments active:**
- Marketing (content production, social distribution)
- Sales (sponsorship outreach)
- Support (subscriber inbox triage)

**What the OS does:**
- Drafts two newsletter issues per week, sends to human for sign-off.
- Cross-posts to LinkedIn, X, Threads with platform-appropriate framing.
- Identifies sponsorship leads weekly, drafts pitches, schedules the sends.
- Reads `agent@blankcollar.ai`, replies to common subscriber questions, escalates the unusual ones.

**What stays human:** the writing voice (sign-off on every issue), the *no* on bad sponsors, the relationships.

## 2. The Two-Person SaaS

**Who:** Founder + designer building a B2B SaaS, $14k MRR, growing 9% MoM.

**Top-level goal:** *"Reach $50k MRR with the same two humans."*

**Departments active:**
- Sales (lead qualification, follow-ups, demo scheduling)
- Support (tier-1 ticket replies, doc updates)
- Finance (invoice processing, MRR reporting, expense categorisation)
- Engineering (changelog, release notes, dependabot triage)

**What the OS does:**
- Watches the `support@` inbox, answers FAQs, drafts replies for everything else.
- Maintains a current-state "company snapshot" the founders read with morning coffee.
- Pays vendors, files receipts, surfaces anomalies for approval.
- Updates the changelog and pings customers when their requested feature ships.

**What stays human:** product decisions, hiring (when it eventually happens), customer calls.

## 3. The Family Business

**Who:** A husband-and-wife agency (designer + project manager) running 6 clients.

**Top-level goal:** *"Take Fridays off without dropping clients."*

**Departments active:**
- Project Ops (status reports, meeting agendas, client-facing updates)
- Finance (invoices, time tracking summaries)
- Marketing (case studies, portfolio updates)

**What the OS does:**
- Sends Friday-morning client updates with what shipped and what's next.
- Drafts the next month's roadmap from this month's notes; humans approve.
- Generates and sends invoices on the 1st and 15th, chases overdue ones politely.

**What stays human:** the actual design work, client phone calls, judgement on tone.

---

## 4. The Creator-Studio

**Who:** A YouTuber with 380k subs, one editor, and one part-time community manager.

**Top-level goal:** *"Cross 1M subs without burning out."*

**Departments active:**
- Content Ops (script research, thumbnail A/B planning, upload checklist)
- Community (Discord moderation queue, comment triage, sponsor-DM responses)
- Sponsorship (incoming pitches → CRM, brand-fit scoring, response drafts)

**What the OS does:**
- Triages 4,000 weekly Discord messages into "needs human" / "auto-reply" / "ignore."
- Maintains a rolling list of script ideas pulled from comments and current events.
- Scores incoming sponsorship emails against the creator's brand-fit rules and proposes a yes/no.

**What stays human:** the camera, the script voice, the final brand decisions.

---

## 5. The Local Service Business

**Who:** A two-location dog-grooming shop. The owner is great with dogs, less great with admin.

**Top-level goal:** *"Stop losing 6 hours a week to admin."*

**Departments active:**
- Bookings (appointment confirmations, reminders, rescheduling)
- Marketing (Instagram captions for the cute dogs of the week)
- Finance (daily till reconciliation, weekly P&L summary)

**What the OS does:**
- Confirms tomorrow's appointments by SMS, asks for confirmation, fills cancellations from the waitlist.
- Drafts Instagram captions from photos uploaded by staff.
- Reconciles daily takings against the booking calendar; flags discrepancies.

**What stays human:** the dogs.

---

## What these scenarios have in common

1. The human keeps the **brand voice**, the **judgement calls**, and the **relationships**.
2. The OS takes the **rote**, the **repetitive**, and the **after-hours**.
3. The dashboard's job is to make the human feel **calm**, not informed-of-everything.

If a feature we're building doesn't help one of the personas above sleep better, it's probably the wrong feature for Phase 4.
