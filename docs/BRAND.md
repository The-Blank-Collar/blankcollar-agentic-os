# Brand Guide

This is how Blank Collar should *feel*. Use it for product copy, marketing, agent-facing prompts, and the look of the dashboard.

## The promise (one line)

> **Work is for bots. Life is for humans.**

That's the tagline, the manifesto, and the test. If a feature pushes more work *onto* a human, it fails the test.

## Naming

- **The Blank Collar** — the brand. The state of life where work is delegated.
- **Blank Collar Agentic OS** — the product (this repo).
- **www.blankcollar.ai** — the marketing site (future).
- **agent@blankcollar.ai** — the customer-facing agent inbox (future).

## Voice

- **Calm.** We're selling life back, not hype.
- **Plain.** Founders, parents, makers — not "users." A senior whose grandkid set up the dashboard should follow along.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** We make hard things easy. We don't pretend the hard things weren't hard.
- **Specific.** "Reach 1,000 newsletter subscribers by July" — not "scale your audience."
- **Light, sometimes.** A small joke is fine. A pun in the product name is fine. Two puns per page is too many.

### Words we like

> goal, outcome, plan, agent, hire, department, brain, run, life, owner, calm, quiet, simple

### Words we avoid

> synergy, leverage, unleash, revolutionary, disrupt, hustle, grind, 10x, ninja, rockstar, cutting-edge

### Voice examples

| Don't                                                          | Do                                                              |
|----------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| "Unleash the power of AI for your business."                   | "Hire your first agent. Sleep better tonight."                  |
| "Revolutionary multi-agent platform."                          | "An OS for running a small company without running yourself into the ground." |
| "Boost productivity 10x with our AI."                          | "Get your weekends back."                                       |
| "Configure your autonomous agentic pipeline."                  | "Tell us your goal. We'll handle the work."                     |

## Visual direction (placeholder)

Final palette TBD when the website kicks off in Phase 8. Working principles for any UI we ship in Phases 0–7:

- **Dark by default.** Soft black background (`#0b0d10`), warm off-white text (`#e7eaee`).
- **One accent.** A single, restrained accent colour (currently a muted blue `#7aa7ff`). No gradients-of-the-month.
- **Generous whitespace.** Calm > dense.
- **Rounded but not playful.** 12–16 px corner radius. No bubble buttons.
- **Type:** system stack until we pick a brand face. `-apple-system, system-ui, sans-serif`.
- **Iconography:** stay close to neutral / line-style. Avoid emoji-as-icon in production UI (READMEs are fine).

## Tone in agent-facing prompts

When Blank Collar speaks *as* an agent to the user, it should sound like a competent, calm chief of staff. Never a sycophant ("Great question!") and never a bro ("Let's crush this goal!").

> "Three options for the email subject line. I think B is the strongest because it matches the open-rate pattern from your last campaign. I'll go with B unless you say otherwise by tomorrow."
