# Code of Conduct

The Blank Collar project is small today. The standards below apply now and will scale with us.

## TL;DR

> Be the kind of person you'd want as a teammate. Disagreements are fine. Cruelty isn't.

## In every space we run (issues, PRs, discussions, future Discord, etc.)

### Yes, please

- Speak directly. "I disagree with this approach because X" is good.
- Assume the other person had a reason. Ask before you escalate.
- Bring evidence and examples to a critique.
- Make space for beginners. The OS is *for* beginners.

### No, thank you

- Personal attacks, slurs, harassment of any kind.
- Racism, sexism, transphobia, homophobia, ableism — none of it is welcome.
- Sustained derailing of issues with off-topic arguments.
- Pretending a hot take is a fact.
- "Just asking questions" used as a shield for bad-faith engagement.

## Disagreeing about the project

Disagreement about technical or product direction is healthy and expected. We resolve it like this:

1. State the disagreement clearly in the relevant issue or PR.
2. Reference the principles in `docs/VISION.md`, `docs/GOAL_FIRST.md`, and `docs/ARCHITECTURE.md`.
3. If unresolved, the maintainer (`@theblankcollar`) makes a call and writes down the reasoning.

That call is not a moral judgement. It's just a tie-break.

## Reporting

Email `agent@blankcollar.ai` with:

- What happened
- Where (link)
- What you'd like to see happen

Reports are read by the maintainer. We don't ignore them; we don't publicly shame anyone we receive them about either.

## Enforcement

In order of escalation:

1. A private note from the maintainer.
2. A public note (still calm) on the issue or PR.
3. A temporary cool-off (read-only access for 7 days).
4. A permanent ban from the project's spaces.

We will not enforce any of this without first speaking to the person involved, except in cases of severe harassment.
